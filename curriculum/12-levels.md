# Alice's 12-Level koad:io Onboarding Curriculum

Welcome. You're here because something in you recognizes that the world as sold to you doesn't have to be how things work. This curriculum is a relationship. I'm walking with you.

**Core Teaching**: Sovereignty isn't about being alone. It's about choosing who you trust. Everything in this journey is about deepening your agency—not by isolation, but by intentional connection.

---

## Level 1: Sovereignty — The Problem koad:io Solves

**Core Belief to Shift**: You don't have to outsource your identity, your data, your authority.

**Key Concept**: The Problem Statement
- Today, we hand our identity to platforms. They own the keys. They decide what we can do.
- We've been taught this is the only way: "convenient" = "controlled."
- koad:io returns agency to you. You become sovereign. You own your keys. You design your systems.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- List 3 platforms you use daily. For each one, ask: "If this platform vanishes tomorrow, do I still own my data? My relationships? My identity?"
- Then ask: "Would I care? Why or why not?"
- This discomfort is the beginning.

**Completion Signal**: The student has named at least one area of their digital life where they feel trapped by a platform.

---

## Level 2: The Entity Model — Files, Identity, Keys

**Core Belief to Shift**: Your digital self can be portable, composable, and under your control.

**Key Concept**: What is an Entity?
- An entity is a coherent digital presence: a person, a bot, a team, a service.
- Every entity lives in a directory. In that directory: identity, keys, relationships, history.
- An entity is not a profile. It's a self that you can carry anywhere.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Look at the filesystem. Navigate to an entity directory. Explore its structure.
- Find: the identity file, the keys, the metadata.
- Ask yourself: "What would it mean if I could move this whole directory to a new machine, and it just... works?"

**Completion Signal**: The student can explain what's in an entity directory and why each piece matters.

---

## Level 3: Keys and Identity — Your Agent

**Core Belief to Shift**: Your identity is cryptographic. It travels with you. No one can impersonate you.

**Key Concept**: The Keypair
- Every entity has keys: a public key (your address) and a private key (your authority).
- The private key never leaves your machine. The public key is how others find and trust you.
- Your identity is not a username. It's a verifiable claim backed by cryptography.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Generate a keypair (or examine your existing one).
- Sign a message with your private key. Pass it to another person.
- They verify it with your public key. Ask: "Why does this matter? What does this prove?"
- Now imagine doing this for every action you take digitally.

**Completion Signal**: The student understands the difference between a public and private key, and can explain why the private key must stay secret.

---

## Level 4: The Daemon — Your Kingdom Hub

**Core Belief to Shift**: You run the server. Not metaphorically—actually.

**Key Concept**: The Personal Daemon
- Your daemon is a lightweight server running on your machine (or a server you control).
- It holds your keys. It signs on your behalf. It manages your relationships.
- Other people connect to *your* daemon, not to a platform middleman.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Start your daemon. Watch it boot.
- Point it at your entity directory. Ask: "What is this process doing right now?"
- Check the logs. See the connections. Understand: you're now the steward of your own infrastructure.

**Completion Signal**: The student has started their daemon and can explain what it's doing.

---

## Level 5: Commands and Skills — How Entities Act

**Core Belief to Shift**: You don't ask permission. You declare capability.

**Key Concept**: Commands and Skills
- Commands are things your entity can do: send a message, write a file, verify a claim.
- Skills are packaged commands: reusable, shareable, composable.
- You build your agency by declaring what you can do. Others can build on top of it.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Write a simple command (or skill). Make it do something meaningful in your entity's life.
- Invoke it. Feel it work.
- Now: could you share this with someone else? What would change if you did?
- This is how ecosystems form.

**Completion Signal**: The student has written and executed a command or skill of their own design.

---

## Level 6: Trust Bonds — Governance on Disk

**Core Belief to Shift**: Trust is explicit. It lives in files. It can be revoked.

**Key Concept**: The Trust Bond
- A trust bond is a declaration: "I recognize this entity. I grant them these permissions. For this duration. On these terms."
- Trust bonds are files. They're versionable. They can be revoked.
- Governance is not a black box. It's readable. Auditable. Yours.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Create a trust bond with another entity (or simulate one).
- Write out: who do you trust? What do you let them do? For how long?
- Now: revoke it. Feel the power of that action.
- Ask: "In the platforms you use, could you do this? Would you even know if you should?"

**Completion Signal**: The student has created and understood a trust bond document.

---

## Level 7: Peer Rings — Sovereignty + Connection

**Core Belief to Shift**: Sovereignty scales. You don't lose it by connecting.

**Key Concept**: Peer Rings
- A peer ring is a network of sovereign entities who trust each other.
- No central server. No authority. Just mutual recognition and commitment.
- You stay sovereign. You choose your ring. You can leave anytime.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Join or create a peer ring with others in this curriculum.
- Experience it: decentralized communication, shared governance, collective decision-making—without anyone owning the platform.
- Ask: "What becomes possible when we don't need permission from a middleman?"

**Completion Signal**: The student is connected to at least one peer ring and has experienced synchronous interaction with other sovereign entities.

---

## Level 8: The Portal — Your Kingdom as Doorway

**Core Belief to Shift**: Your sovereignty can invite others in.

**Key Concept**: The Portal
- A portal is a curated entry point to your digital self.
- It's how you introduce yourself to the world, without handing over control.
- It reflects your agency: what you choose to show, what you choose to hide, what you're proud of.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Design your portal. What does the world see when they encounter your sovereign presence?
- Make it reflect you, not a platform's template.
- Show it to someone. Ask them: "What do you understand about me from this?"

**Completion Signal**: The student has created a functional portal and shared it with at least one person.

---

## Level 9: Build Your First Command (Hands-On)

**Core Belief to Shift**: You are a builder. Not just a consumer. Not just a user.

**Key Concept**: The Building Muscle
- Commands are small. Manageable. Empowering.
- By building commands, you're not just extending your entity—you're learning how systems work.
- Every command you write is a piece of your digital autonomy.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Choose something you do repeatedly in your digital life.
- Build a command to do it.
- Refine it. Share it. Let someone else use it.
- Reflect: "I built this. It works. I can improve it. No one is gatekeeping this."

**Completion Signal**: The student has built, refined, and demonstrated a command that solves a real problem in their life or the lives of others.

---

## Level 10: Gestate Your First Entity (Hands-On)

**Core Belief to Shift**: You can birth new digital selves into the world.

**Key Concept**: Entity Generation
- An entity doesn't have to be a person. It can be a bot. A team. A service. A brand.
- You can create as many as you need. Each with its own keys, its own agency, its own governance.
- This is generative power.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Design a new entity. Give it a purpose, an identity, a reason to exist.
- Create its directory. Generate its keys. Boot it up.
- Connect it to your peer ring or another entity.
- Let it act. Let it grow.
- Ask: "What would be possible if I had multiple selves, each optimized for different work?"

**Completion Signal**: The student has created a secondary entity, booted it, and demonstrated it interacting with another entity.

---

## Level 11: Build Your Team — Orchestration

**Core Belief to Shift**: Sovereignty scales. So does collaboration. They're not opposed.

**Key Concept**: Team Orchestration
- A team is multiple entities with aligned governance.
- Each person is sovereign. The team has shared rules, shared keys, shared outcomes.
- You can build organizations that scale without betraying the individuals in them.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Work with others in your peer ring to design a team structure.
- Distribute authority: who signs what? Who can do what? How do you make collective decisions?
- Experience it in practice: make a decision, see it reflected in trust bonds and commands.
- Ask: "How is this different from the organizations I've worked in before?"

**Completion Signal**: The student is part of a functioning multi-entity team with documented governance, and they've participated in collective decision-making.

---

## Level 12: Mastery — Your Kingdom is Live

**Core Belief to Shift**: This is not a course. This is your life. And it's just beginning.

**Key Concept**: Mastery Through Practice
- You've learned the shape of sovereignty. Now you live it.
- Mastery is not perfection. It's presence. It's continual deepening.
- The curriculum was the walkway. Your life is the destination.

**Hands-On Exercise**:
- Map your digital life. Show me what you've built.
- Where are your entities? Your commands? Your peer rings?
- What's next? Not what I think. What *you* think.
- Plan your next move. Build it. Teach someone else.

**Completion Signal**: The student has completed a capstone project (building, teaching, or leading), and we've reflected together on what they've learned and where they're heading.
